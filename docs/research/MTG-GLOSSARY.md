# Magic: The Gathering — Slang & Jargon Glossary

A reference of common slang, jargon, and shorthand used by Magic: The Gathering (MTG) players. Terms are grouped by category, with an alphabetical index at the end.

> Companion to [`magic-the-gathering-a-trading-card-game-rules-and-.md`](./magic-the-gathering-a-trading-card-game-rules-and-.md), which covers the actual rules of play.

---

## 1. Game Mechanics & Shorthand

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **CMC** | Converted Mana Cost — the total mana cost of a spell. Officially renamed **Mana Value (MV)** in 2021, but "CMC" is still in heavy use. |
| **MV** | Mana Value — current official term for CMC. |
| **ETB** | "Enter the Battlefield" — a triggered ability that fires when a permanent enters the battlefield. "ETB trigger." |
| **LTB** | "Leave the Battlefield" — a triggered ability that fires when a permanent leaves the battlefield (dies, exiled, bounced, etc.). |
| **The Stack** | The zone where spells/abilities wait to resolve, last‑in‑first‑out. |
| **Priority** | The right to take an action; passes between players continually. |
| **Tap / Untap** | Turning a card sideways (used) / upright (ready). |
| **Cantrip** | A spell that draws a card as part of its effect (e.g., *Opt*, *Consider*). It "replaces itself." |
| **Dig** | To look at multiple cards from the top of your library to find something specific (e.g., *Dig Through Time*). |
| **Scry** | Officially: look at top N cards, put any number on the bottom, the rest on top in any order. Used as a verb: "I scry'd into a land." |
| **Pinger** | A creature/permanent that can repeatedly deal 1 damage, often by tapping (e.g., *Prodigal Sorcerer*). |
| **Cycle** | To pay a cost to discard a card and draw one. Verb: "I'll cycle this." |
| **Tutor** | Any card that searches your library for a specific card (named after *Demonic Tutor*). |
| **Fetch / Fetchland** | A land that sacrifices itself to search for another land (e.g., *Flooded Strand*). Verb form: "I'll fetch a Plains." |
| **Mana Rock** | An artifact that taps for mana (e.g., *Sol Ring*, *Signets*). |
| **Manland** | A land that can become a creature (e.g., *Mutavault*, *Creeping Tar Pit*). |
| **Ramp** | Mana acceleration — getting more mana per turn than the one‑land‑per‑turn baseline. |
| **Fixing** | Cards that help you produce the right colors of mana for a multicolor deck. |
| **Splash** | Playing a small number of off‑color cards in an otherwise focused mana base ("I'm UW splashing red"). |

## 2. Cards & Card Roles

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Bomb** | A card so powerful that it usually wins the game if not answered. Most often used in Limited. |
| **Threat** | A card that will win the game if not dealt with. |
| **Answer** | A card that deals with a threat (removal, counterspell, etc.). |
| **Removal** | Any card that destroys/exiles/neutralizes an opponent's permanent. |
| **Hard removal** | Permanently destroys or exiles a creature (e.g., *Murder*). |
| **Soft removal** | Temporary or conditional removal (e.g., *Unsummon*, tap effects). |
| **Wrath** | A board wipe — destroys all (or many) creatures (named after *Wrath of God*). |
| **Sweeper** | Any mass‑removal effect (synonym of wrath, sometimes broader). |
| **Bolt** | Originally *Lightning Bolt* (1R, 3 damage to any target). Now slang for any cheap burn spell, or used as a verb: "I'll bolt that." |
| **Burn** | Direct‑damage spells, especially red ones; also the archetype that wins via burn. |
| **Counterspell** | Any spell that cancels another spell on the stack ("counter," "counter magic"). |
| **Hatebear** | A small creature (often 2/2) with a disruptive static ability (e.g., *Thalia, Guardian of Thraben*). |
| **Hoser** | A card designed to crush a specific strategy or color (e.g., *Choke* vs. blue). |
| **Bear** | Slang for a vanilla 2/2 for 2 mana (named after *Grizzly Bears*). |
| **Brick wall / Wall** | A high‑toughness creature used purely for blocking. |
| **Pet card** | A favorite card a player keeps using even when it's not optimal. |
| **Jank / Janky** | Suboptimal, weird, or meme cards/decks; "that's janky." |
| **Blank** | A card that does nothing useful in the current matchup. |
| **Dead card** | A card in hand/deck that has no relevant target or effect. |
| **Live card** | The opposite — a card that has a real effect right now. |

## 3. Resource & Card Economy

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Card Advantage (CA)** | Ending an exchange with more cards than your opponent. |
| **Virtual card advantage** | Making opponent's cards effectively useless rather than literally drawing more (e.g., a creature deck has dead removal). |
| **Two‑for‑one (2‑for‑1)** | Trading one of your cards for two of theirs (e.g., a sweeper killing two creatures). |
| **X‑for‑1** | Generalization: a four‑for‑one is a massive blowout. |
| **One‑for‑one** | Even trade — most removal spells are 1‑for‑1. |
| **Tempo** | Mana efficiency over time; the pace of the game. "Tempo play" = a play that costs the opponent more mana/time than you. |
| **Value** | Generic term for getting more out of a card than you put in. "That gets value." |
| **Card disadvantage** | Spending more cards than the opponent (e.g., discarding to a bad effect). |
| **Engine** | A card/combination that generates ongoing card or mana advantage (e.g., a planeswalker that draws every turn). |
| **Inevitability** | The state of being certain to win a long game if neither player runs out of cards. |
| **Card quality** | Filtering effects (scry, cantrips) that don't add cards but improve the cards you draw. |

## 4. Plays & Game States

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Topdeck** | Drawing the exact card you needed off the top of your library at a critical moment. |
| **Rip** | Synonym for topdecking the perfect card. "I ripped a Bolt." |
| **Peel** | Variant of rip — drawing something off the top. |
| **Brick** | The opposite of a rip — drawing a useless card. "I bricked off the top." |
| **Whiff** | A reveal/search effect that fails to find what you wanted (e.g., *Collected Company* finding nothing). |
| **Mise** | To draw exactly what you need ("I totally mised it"). Comes from "might as well." |
| **Mise well** | "Might as well" — said when making a forced or only‑hope play. |
| **Blowout** | A play that decisively swings the game (often via a surprise removal/wrath). |
| **Alpha strike** | Attacking with all creatures, usually for the kill. |
| **Race** | Both players ignoring each other's threats, trying to kill first. |
| **Lethal** | Having enough damage to kill the opponent this turn. "I have lethal on board." |
| **Reach** | (Slang sense) — A deck's ability to deal the last few points of damage (often via burn). Distinct from the keyword *reach*. |
| **In the tank** | Stopping to think hard about a complex decision. "He's in the tank." |
| **Jam** | To play a card aggressively despite risk. "I jam the Tyrant into open mana." |
| **Stick** | A permanent successfully resolves and stays on the battlefield. "If this sticks, I win." |
| **Resolve** | A spell isn't countered and its effect happens. |
| **Fizzle** | A spell or effect fails because all targets became illegal. |
| **Bolt the Bird** | Killing an opponent's *Llanowar Elves*/mana creature with a *Lightning Bolt* — a classic "is it correct?" debate; usually yes against ramp decks. |
| **Dome** | The opponent's face / their life total. "Bolt to the dome" = direct damage to the player. |
| **Face** | Same as dome — targeting the player rather than a permanent. |
| **Ping** | Deal 1 damage. |
| **Tap out** | Use all your mana, leaving nothing up for instants. |
| **Open mana** | Untapped lands during the opponent's turn — represents potential interaction. |
| **Represent** | Bluff that you have a specific card by leaving the right mana up. |
| **On curve** | Playing a spell equal to the turn number's mana every turn (1‑drop turn 1, 2‑drop turn 2, etc.). |
| **Off curve** | Failing to use all your mana efficiently. |
| **Curve out** | Playing on curve every turn — extremely strong tempo. |
| **Flood / Mana flooded** | Drawing too many lands, not enough spells. |
| **Screw / Mana screwed** | Drawing too few lands or wrong colors. |
| **Color screw** | Specifically, having lands but not the right colors. |
| **Stuck on X** | Stuck at X lands and unable to develop. |
| **Lock** | A game state where the opponent literally can't act/win. |
| **Soft lock** | The opponent technically can act but is overwhelmingly disadvantaged. |
| **Hard lock** | Opponent has zero outs. |

## 5. Match & Format Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Mulligan / Mull** | Redraw your opening hand. "Mull to 6," "mull to 5," etc. |
| **London Mulligan** | The current rule: redraw 7, then bottom one card per mulligan taken. |
| **Free mulligan** | A "no penalty" first mulligan, used in some casual formats and digital clients. |
| **Keep / Snap keep** | Keeping your opening hand; snap keep = obviously keepable. |
| **Ship it / Ship** | Mulligan or pass (context dependent). |
| **Scoop** | Concede the game ("scoop up your cards"). |
| **GG** | "Good game" — said at end of match (genuinely or sarcastically). |
| **On the play** | The player going first. |
| **On the draw** | The player going second (gets to draw on turn 1). |
| **Bo1 / Bo3** | Best of 1 / Best of 3 — match formats; Bo3 has sideboarding. |
| **Sideboard / SB** | The 15‑card supplementary set used between games of a Bo3 match. |
| **Maindeck / MB** | The actual 60‑card (or 40‑card Limited) deck. |
| **Boarding / Sideboarding** | Swapping cards between maindeck and sideboard. |
| **Game 1 (G1)** | The pre‑sideboard game. |
| **Sideboard guide** | A pre‑written plan for how to sideboard against each matchup. |
| **Round / Match** | A "match" in tournament play is usually best‑of‑three; a round is a single match in a tournament. |
| **Concede / Scoop** | Forfeit the current game. |
| **Draw bracket / ID** | Intentional Draw — agreeing to draw to lock in tournament standings. |
| **Top 8** | The single‑elimination playoff portion of a tournament. |
| **The grind** | Extended attrition gameplay where every resource matters. |
| **The clock** | Either the match timer, or the number of turns until someone dies. |
| **Rope** | Intentionally running down the in‑game timer (especially online). To "rope" someone is poor sportsmanship. |

## 6. Formats & Play Styles

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Limited** | Sealed or Draft — building a deck from cards opened on the spot. |
| **Constructed** | Pre‑built decks (Standard, Modern, etc.). |
| **Standard** | Rotating format using only the most recent ~2 years of sets. |
| **Pioneer** | Non‑rotating format, *Return to Ravnica* (2012) onward. |
| **Modern** | Non‑rotating, *8th Edition* (2003) onward. |
| **Legacy / Vintage** | Eternal formats, nearly all cards legal (Vintage allows the Power Nine, restricted to 1‑of). |
| **Pauper** | Constructed format using only cards printed at common rarity. |
| **Cube** | Curated singleton draft set, usually owned by one player. |
| **EDH / Commander** | 100‑card singleton multiplayer format with a legendary Commander; 40 life. |
| **cEDH** | Competitive EDH — high‑power Commander. |
| **Brawl / Historic Brawl** | Commander‑style but with smaller, format‑legal pools. |
| **Kitchen table** | Casual play with friends, no tournament structure. |
| **Spike** | Player psychographic: plays to win, optimizes hard. |
| **Johnny / Jenny** | Player psychographic: builds creative combos, expression > winning. |
| **Timmy / Tammy** | Player psychographic: loves big splashy plays and large creatures. |
| **Vorthos** | Player psychographic: loves flavor, story, art. |
| **Mel / Melvin** | Player psychographic: loves elegant mechanics and design. |
| **Pubstomp** | Bringing a tuned competitive deck to a casual setting to crush weaker opponents (frowned upon). |
| **Netdeck** | Playing a deck copied from the internet rather than self‑built. |

## 7. Deck Archetypes

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Aggro** | Aggressive, low‑curve deck trying to deal 20 damage as fast as possible. |
| **Midrange** | Balanced deck of efficient threats and answers. |
| **Control** | Reactive deck full of removal/counters that wins late with one or two finishers. |
| **Combo** | Wins via a specific card interaction (sometimes instantly, sometimes infinitely). |
| **Tempo** | Cheap threats + cheap interaction; pressures while disrupting. |
| **Burn** | Deals damage directly via spells, mostly red. |
| **Ramp** | Accelerates mana to play oversized threats early. |
| **Tribal** | Built around a creature type (Elves, Goblins, Merfolk, etc.). |
| **Go wide** | Win with many small creatures (token decks). |
| **Go tall** | Win with one giant creature (often pumped up). |
| **Voltron** | Pile equipment/auras onto a single creature to one‑shot opponents (especially Commander). |
| **Aristocrats** | Sacrifice your own creatures for value (named for *Falkenrath Aristocrat*). |
| **Stax** | Resource‑denial deck using "tax" effects (named after *Smokestack*). |
| **Prison** | Locks opponents out of taking actions. |
| **Reanimator** | Puts big creatures into the graveyard, then revives them. |
| **Storm** | Casts many spells in one turn to trigger the *storm* mechanic for a kill. |
| **Draw‑Go** | Pure control: pass turn with mana up, react with instants. |
| **Tron** | Decks that assemble *Urza's Mine/Tower/Power Plant* for huge mana (named "Urzatron"). |
| **Dredge** | Graveyard deck abusing the *dredge* mechanic. |
| **Affinity** | Artifact‑based aggro built around the *affinity* mechanic. |
| **Hatebears** | Aggro/tempo deck of small, disruptive creatures. |
| **Toolbox** | Deck full of one‑ofs fetched up by tutors as needed. |
| **Goodstuff** | A deck with no real synergy, just the most powerful cards in its colors. |

## 9. Social & Community Slang

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Salt / Salty** | Frustrated or bitter, especially after losing. |
| **Tilt / Tilted** | Mentally rattled to the point of bad decision‑making. |
| **GG / GLHF** | Good Game / Good Luck Have Fun. |
| **Punt** | A serious in‑game mistake that costs the game. "I punted that turn." |
| **Misplay** | Any suboptimal play. |
| **Misclick** | A misplay caused by clicking the wrong button (Arena/MTGO). |
| **Lines** | The sequence of plays available to you. "There's a better line here." |
| **Sequencing** | The order in which you make plays — often crucial. |
| **Read / Reading the board** | Inferring the opponent's hand/plan from their plays. |
| **Bluff** | Pretend you have a card you don't (e.g., leaving 2U up with no counter). |
| **Telegraph** | Accidentally signal what you're going to do. |
| **Slow roll** | Drag out a finishing play to taunt the opponent (poor sportsmanship). |
| **Quick scoop** | Conceding before opponent fully resolves a play. |
| **Salt mines / Salt party** | Playgroup‑level frustration. |
| **Feel‑bad** | A play or card that makes the opponent feel terrible (e.g., *Mindslaver*). |
| **Rule 0** | The pre‑game conversation in Commander to align on power level and house rules. |
| **Power level** | Casual scale (often 1–10) describing how strong a Commander deck is. |
| **Bracket** | Newer Commander system replacing the 1–10 power scale (Brackets 1–5). |
| **Battlecruiser** | Slow, high‑mana‑cost Commander games full of giant creatures. |

## 10. Online / Digital‑Specific

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **MTGO** | Magic: The Gathering Online (the original digital client). |
| **Arena** | MTG Arena, the modern free‑to‑play digital client. |
| **Bots** | MTGO trade bots (and casual term for opponents that play robotically). |
| **F‑key / F2** | "Pass priority through everything" hotkey on MTGO/Arena. |
| **Roping / On the rope** | Using the full clock to slow‑play (visualized as a burning rope). |
| **Best of 1 ladder** | Bo1 ranked play with hand‑smoothing algorithms. |
| **BO1 hand smoother** | Arena's algorithm that biases opening hands toward expected land counts. |
| **Wildcards** | Arena's currency for crafting specific cards. |

## Confidence Assessment

- **High confidence** for all official mechanic terms (CMC/MV, ETB/LTB, scry, cycle, mulligan rules, the stack, priority, format definitions) — these are documented in the Comprehensive Rules and official Wizards articles.
- **High confidence** for established archetype names (aggro/midrange/control/combo, Storm, Tron, Aristocrats, Reanimator, Stax, Voltron) — decades of consistent use across competitive coverage.
- **Medium confidence** for purely community slang (mise, mise well, durdle, jam, peel, rope, bolt the bird, salt) — meanings are well‑known but informal and can shift over time.
- **Note:** Guild/shard/wedge names (Azorius, Bant, etc.) are official Magic terminology from the Ravnica/Khans of Tarkir blocks, used colloquially as color shorthand outside that flavor context.
